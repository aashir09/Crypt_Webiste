Our Proprietary Construction Technology

CDC manufactures affordable prefabricated homes in its own facilities through its proprietary technology, “nexus”. These facilities are specialized factories where prefab homes are manufactured in a controlled environment using “nexus”, before being transported to their final location for assembly. Our facilities are equipped with advanced machinery and skilled labor to efficiently produce high-quality affordable homes. The “nexus” process begins with the design phase, where architects and engineers create customized plans based on the client's specifications. Once the design is finalized, the manufacturing process begins. This typically involves constructing the various components of the home, such as walls, roofs and floors in separate sections.

Each component is built to exact specifications using precision machinery, ensuring consistency and quality. Once all the components are completed, they are transported to the site where they are assembled into a finished home. This assembly process is quick and efficient, minimizing construction time and costs. CDC homes built with nexus offer several advantages over traditional construction, including lower costs, faster build times, and improved quality control. As such, prefabricated house manufacturing facilities play a crucial role in the modern construction industry, offering a sustainable and efficient solution to the growing demand for affordable housing.

Having a technology like nexus at our disposal provides us a competitive edge as a supplier focusing on affordable housing, enabling us to offer cost-effective, high-quality, and customizable housing solutions to a wide range of customers.

Cost Efficiency:

By producing nexus components in-house, we reduce manufacturing costs compared to outsourcing. This cost savings can be passed on to customers, making the housing more affordable.

Quality Control:

With an in-house manufacturing facility, we have a greater control over the quality of the materials and construction processes. This can result in higher-quality housing that meets or exceeds industry standards.

Faster Construction:

"nexus" components are produced in a controlled environment simultaneously with site preparation, reducing construction time significantly. This faster construction process is helping us meet the growing demand for affordable housing more quickly.

Customization:

Having a manufacturing facility allows CDC to offer more customization options to customers. This helps meet the diverse needs of different communities and demographics, making the housing more attractive and marketable.

Sustainability:

Prefabricated construction is more sustainable than traditional construction methods, as it generates less waste and incorporates environmentally friendly materials and practices. This appeals to environmentally conscious customers and helps us meet sustainability goals.

Scalability:

CDC with its own manufacturing facility has the ability to scale production up or down based on demand. This flexibility helps us manage costs and ensure that housing remains affordable even as demand fluctuates.

Revolutionizing the Affordable Housing Investments through Crypto
